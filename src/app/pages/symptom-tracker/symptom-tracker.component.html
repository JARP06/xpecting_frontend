Pregnancy Symptoms to Consult Your Doctor About

If you're experiencing any of the following pregnancy symptoms, it's important to consult your doctor:

  • Vaginal Bleeding: While some spotting can be normal, heavy bleeding may indicate a problem.
  • Severe Abdominal Pain: Sharp or persistent abdominal pain could signal an issue like ectopic pregnancy or miscarriage.
  • Severe Nausea and Vomiting: Persistent vomiting and inability to keep fluids down may lead to dehydration.
  • Decreased Fetal Movement: If you notice a significant decrease in fetal movement, contact your doctor immediately.
  • Severe Headaches or Visual Disturbances: These could be signs of preeclampsia, a serious condition.
  • Sudden Swelling: Swelling in your hands, feet, or face can be a sign of preeclampsia.
  • Difficulty Breathing: Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing could indicate a problem like pulmonary embolism.
  • Chills or Fever: High fever during pregnancy can be harmful to both you and your baby.
  • Severe Itching: Especially if it's accompanied by nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, or jaundice, it could indicate a liver problem.
  • Persistent Contractions: Regular contractions before 37 weeks could be a sign of preterm labor.
